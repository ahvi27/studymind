Biology Study Notes - Cell Structure and Function

Chapter 1: Introduction to Cells

All living organisms are composed of cells. Cells are the basic unit of life. There are two main types of cells:
1. Prokaryotic cells - lack a nucleus (bacteria)
2. Eukaryotic cells - have a nucleus (plants, animals, fungi)

The cell membrane is a phospholipid bilayer that controls what enters and exits the cell. It contains proteins that act as channels, pumps, and receptors.

The nucleus contains DNA and controls cell activities. The nucleolus produces ribosomes.

Mitochondria are the powerhouse of the cell. They produce ATP through cellular respiration. The equation is:
C6H12O6 + 6O2 -> 6CO2 + 6H2O + ATP

The endoplasmic reticulum (ER) comes in two forms:
- Rough ER: has ribosomes, synthesizes proteins
- Smooth ER: synthesizes lipids, detoxifies drugs

The Golgi apparatus modifies, sorts, and packages proteins for secretion.

Lysosomes contain digestive enzymes that break down waste materials.

Chapter 2: Cell Division

Mitosis is the process of cell division that produces two identical daughter cells. The phases are:
1. Prophase - chromosomes condense
2. Metaphase - chromosomes align at the equator
3. Anaphase - sister chromatids separate
4. Telophase - nuclear membranes reform
5. Cytokinesis - cytoplasm divides

Meiosis produces four genetically different haploid cells and is used for sexual reproduction.

Key differences between mitosis and meiosis:
- Mitosis: 1 division, 2 diploid cells, identical offspring
- Meiosis: 2 divisions, 4 haploid cells, genetic variation

Chapter 3: Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis occurs in chloroplasts and converts light energy to chemical energy.

6CO2 + 6H2O + light -> C6H12O6 + 6O2

The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes and produce ATP and NADPH.
The Calvin cycle occurs in the stroma and fixes carbon dioxide into glucose.

Factors affecting photosynthesis: light intensity, CO2 concentration, temperature, water availability.
