World History Notes - The Industrial Revolution

Overview

The Industrial Revolution (1760-1840) was a period of major industrialization that began in Great Britain and spread throughout the world. It transformed economies from agrarian and handicraft-based to machine manufacturing.

Causes of the Industrial Revolution

1. Agricultural Revolution - improved farming techniques increased food production
2. Population growth - provided labor force and market demand
3. Natural resources - coal and iron ore in Britain
4. Capital accumulation - wealth from trade and colonialism
5. Technological innovation - steam engine, spinning jenny, power loom
6. Political stability - relatively peaceful conditions in Britain

Key Inventions

1712 - Thomas Newcomen: atmospheric steam engine
1764 - James Hargreaves: spinning jenny
1769 - James Watt: improved steam engine
1785 - Edmund Cartwright: power loom
1807 - Robert Fulton: steamboat
1825 - George Stephenson: first passenger railway

Social Impacts

Urbanization: people moved from rural areas to cities for factory work.
Working conditions were often dangerous with long hours (12-16 hours/day) and low wages.
Child labor was common until reform movements emerged.

The Luddites (1811-1816) destroyed machinery they believed threatened their jobs.

Positive impacts included rising living standards over time, growth of middle class, and improved transportation.

Economic Changes

Factory system replaced cottage industry.
Mass production lowered costs of goods.
New economic theories emerged:
- Adam Smith: free market capitalism (Wealth of Nations, 1776)
- Karl Marx: criticized capitalism, predicted proletariat revolution

Spread to Other Countries

Belgium (early 1800s), France, Germany (mid-1800s), United States (late 1800s), Japan (Meiji Restoration, 1868).

Second Industrial Revolution (1870-1914) featured steel, electricity, chemicals, and petroleum.

Key Figures

James Watt - steam engine improvements
Henry Bessemer - steel production process
Andrew Carnegie - American steel industry
Thomas Edison - electric light bulb and power systems

Exam Review Questions

Q: Why did the Industrial Revolution start in Britain?
A: Britain had coal, iron, capital, colonies, stable government, and entrepreneurial culture.

Q: What was the impact on the environment?
A: Air and water pollution from factories, deforestation for fuel and construction.

Q: How did it affect women?
A: Many women worked in textile factories; later domestic ideology emerged for middle-class women.
